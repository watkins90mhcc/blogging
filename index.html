If asked to name the first computer I could remember using it would have to be the Tandy Color Computer. Though since I was very young I hanve only one or two memories at the most about it. The game as seen in the carousel is the one of the games that I can remember sitting on the edege of my parents old water bed and playing, and hearing those infamous three words when one of the bugs managed to find you "We Got Ya." Super Nintendo Entertainment System
SNES Photo has been modified

If you were a young kid in the 90's no doubt you were in someway exposed to Nintendo's 16 bit console, simply called the Super Nintendo. A new console, a new controller, Mode 7, FX Chip, the ability to play 32-bit gaming experience on 16-bit hardware, and more. This console also fought side-by-side against Sega's 16-bit console the Sega Genesis in a battle of Supremacy for what came to be known as the Bit Wars. My experiences with this system came only with visits to my grandparent's house, because it would be quite a few years before I would have a console of my own.

Sega Genesis

Released a year before the Super Nintendo Sega's new console brought people their first 16-bit experiences, and saw the birth of one of the world's most beloved characters next to Nintendo's Mario, Sonic the Hedgehog. Add ons like the 32X would mark Sega's attempt to bring 32-bit gaming experiences to their console, and the Sega CD would allow Genesis owner's to play CD format games through their console. The ability to download games is something we take for granted now-a-days; however, many Genesis owners got to experience the first ever download and play service called Sega Channel.
